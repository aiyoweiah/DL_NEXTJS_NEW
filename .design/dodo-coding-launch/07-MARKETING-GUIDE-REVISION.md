## In one paragraph

The marketing guide v1.0 is built around **The Loop** (Read → Reason → Speak → Decide) as the named methodology and the brand's most marketable asset. The user has retired The Loop from marketing surfaces. This revision rewrites the marketing guide v1.0 → v1.1 around the new framing: **DODO Coding is a language art for the AI age; we teach how AI reads, thinks, and writes; critical thinking is the human discipline emphasized throughout.** No methodology name. No fourth verb. The pedagogical substance — AI4K12 mapping, four open pillars, Navigator model, 16-week cycle, "we cite our sources" credibility — survives intact; it's unbundled from the Loop wrapper.

## Sections that need rewriting

These are the heavy edits — substance change, not word-level polish.

### §1.2 — The brand promise

**v1.0 (current):**
> *DODO Learning ELA teaches a child to think precisely in human language.*
> *DODO Coding teaches a child to think precisely in machine language — and to know when only a human should decide.*
> *One mind, two literacies. The graduate of both is the child best equipped for the AI age: fluent in what machines do, critical about when they should.*

**Problems with v1.0:**
- Leans on the ELA-parallel claim (retired per user direction)
- The fourth-verb closing ("only a human should decide") references the Decide verb that's also retired

**v1.1 (proposed):**
> *DODO Coding is a language art for the AI age. We teach a child how AI reads, thinks, and writes — and how to think critically about what AI does. Machine fluency at the literacy level. Critical thinking as the discipline that gives it value.*

**Rationale:** Drops the parallel claim. Names the category ("a language art"). Names the substance (three machine verbs). Names the human discipline (critical thinking) as an emphasis, not a fourth phase. Preserves the "machine fluency / critical thinking" pairing the original brand promise was selling.

**Rationale:** The primary tagline is now the category claim, not a verb-pair couplet. The sub-tagline does the substance work. The alternates surface different facets without falling back to the Loop-shaped four-verb structure.

### §1.4 — Audience positioning line

**v1.0 (current):**
> *For children who will build with machines — and decide what is worth building.*

**v1.1 (proposed):**
> *For children who will work with AI every day of their lives — and need to think critically about what it produces.*

**Rationale:** Drops "decide what is worth building" (the Decide verb echo). Names the actual lived experience (working with AI daily) and the actual discipline (critical thinking about output). Resonates with parents who already see their child using AI tools — closer to the parent's existing mental model.

### §3 — The DODO Coding Loop (entire section retired and replaced)

**v1.0 (current):** Entire §3 is "The DODO Coding Loop." Names Read → Reason → Speak → Decide as the methodology. Maps to AI4K12. Calls itself "the single most marketable asset" (§3.2).

**v1.1 (proposed):** Section renamed to **§3 — What we teach**. New structure:

- **§3.1 — The three machine abilities we teach.** Names Read, Think, Write as the three things AI does. Maps each to AI4K12 Big Idea (1 / 2+3 / 4). Names the tools (CMU CS Academy for Read+Think; Claude/Gemini for Write). Each ability gets a short copy block in the marketing register, written the way `/coding/methodology` will render it.

- **§3.2 — Critical thinking: the human discipline.** Maps to AI4K12 Big Idea 5 + Toulmin. Frames critical thinking as the emphasis layered through every session, not a fourth phase. Names "override conditions," "verify every output," "question what AI says" as the specific skills the student practices.

- **§3.3 — Why this is a language art.** New subsection. Argues the category claim: just as English language arts teaches how language is read, thought, spoken, and written, DODO Coding teaches how machine language is read, thought, and written — with the human's critical thinking as the discipline that anchors literacy in either language. Acknowledges this is a positioning move; does not claim brand-architecture parallel with DODO ELA.

- **§3.4 — How this maps to the four pillars.** Same content as v1.0 §3.1 (the table mapping Loop phases to pillars), reshaped: each pillar's role is named without reference to a Loop phase. AI4K12 = curriculum map; CMU CS Academy = technical spine; Huyen AI Engineering = age-adapted AI-era textbook; Claude/Gemini = ambient lab environment.

**Retired entirely from §3:**
- The "Every session. Every week. In this order, without exception." opener (it was the Loop's rhythm; preserved in spirit by the §4 brand voice "every session" copy convention, not as a section opener)
- The four-phase walkthrough (replaced by §3.1 three-ability + §3.2 critical-thinking)
- The §3.2 "single most marketable asset" claim (the asset is now the language-art positioning, not a four-phase graphic)

### §5.2 — Headline stats template

**v1.0 (current):**
> *2 → 4 average AI4K12 mastery growth across all five Big Ideas in 16 weeks*

**Problem:** "Across all five Big Ideas" implies the methodology touches all five evenly. Under the new framing, the three machine verbs map to Big Ideas 1–4; Big Idea 5 is the critical-thinking emphasis. The stat line should reflect that split.

**v1.1 (proposed):**
> *2 → 4 average AI4K12 mastery growth across Big Ideas 1–4 in 16 weeks · concurrent Big Idea 5 (Societal Impact) growth tracked separately via the critical-thinking rubric*

**Rationale:** Honest. Reflects how the curriculum actually teaches. Anchors the Big Idea 5 stat as a separate track for parents who want to see the critical-thinking growth specifically.

### Appendix A — Glossary for the writing team

**v1.0 entries to retire:**
- "The Loop" (renamed/retired — no longer the named methodology)
- "Decide" (retired — no longer a phase name)
- "Read → Reason → Speak → Decide" (retired — replaced by Read · Think · Write triplet + critical-thinking emphasis)
- "Speak" as machine-output verb (retired — replaced by Write)
- "Reason" as machine-processing verb (retired — replaced by Think)

**v1.1 entries to add:**
- **A language art for the AI age** — DODO Coding's category claim. Always this phrase, used as positioning. Eyebrow or sub-tagline.
- **The three machine abilities** — Read · Think · Write. The substance DODO Coding teaches. Always presented as a plain triplet (no arrows, no graphic asset).
- **Critical thinking (DODO Coding sense)** — the human discipline DODO Coding cultivates: question what AI says, verify outputs, decide when not to trust. Grounded in AI4K12 Big Idea 5 + Toulmin. Always paired with a framework name when used.
- **Override conditions** — engineering vocabulary for "when should the human stop the agent?" — borrowed from advanced cycle A3. Used in body copy as a concrete example of critical thinking, not as a phase name.

**v1.1 entries that stay unchanged:**
- Navigator (capitalized; specialist not tutor)
- 16 weeks / sixteen weeks
- AI4K12 Five Big Ideas (cite ai4k12.org)
- CMU CS Academy (proper name; link academy.cs.cmu.edu)
- Foundation models (preferred over "large language models")
